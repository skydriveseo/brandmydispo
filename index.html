Mylar Bags DIY- Print on It at Home, All by Yourself



You're attempting to print on mylar bags and sachets at home but are unsure of which printing process to use or how to print. Here, we'll go through how to imprint on mylar bags at home step by step. We'll go through how to do it yourself so you can simply print mylar bags at homes for a minimal cost.

Custom mylar bags are thin and may be used to store food grains or other objects that need to be protected from air, moisture, & light. Aluminum foil or polyamide film is used to prepare the bags. If you are starting a business and wish to sell things under your brand's banner, or if you are a product decorating shop, you will need to print custom mylar bags.

Follow the below-mentioned steps properly to print your bags easily.

1. Printing the Design

To prepare the design, utilize the product design tools such as Coreldraw or Adobe Photoshop.

Use an inkjet transparency film or an inkjet printer to print the design. Make sure to reflect the picture to get the proper printing format. Above all, configure the printer to high quality.

2. Making the Silkscreen

You may use either a wooden or an aluminum frame for this technique. In general, wooden is preferable since it is less expensive and more suited to home-based printing needs. In addition, you will use DIY screen printing methods that need steps, eliminating ready-made purchases from the regional supermarket. As a result, you should prepare the panels at home. The following is the silkscreen preparation procedure:

Put the silkscreen to the supporting structure first. A fine mesh screen will be used. Connect the two adjoining sides of the screen to the wooden base first. Then join the other two sides. You will generally utilize a claw hammer, which is often used in carpentry, to attach the screen to the frame.

3. Applying the Photo Emulsion

Mix the adsorbent or diazo activator with the picture emulsion before putting it on the screens. To make the colloid light sensitive, you will have to add an adsorbent or activator. The emulsion-to-sensitizer ratio should be 9:1. That is, combine 90% phot emulsion and 10% activator. The amount is determined by your manufacturing needs. Furthermore, if you are only printing a few bags, such as 2 or 3 mylar bags, then you can mix a cup of the solution.

4. Preparing for Printing

You will have to note down these steps to print the design:

  • Take a soft platen (usually a wooden board) of appropriate dimensions (length & breadth) for imprinting mylar bags.
  • Draw the bag through into the pad to create a level printing surface.
  • Then, using a ball pen, center the design positively on the mylar bag and mark its position on the screen.
  • Now, use cooking oil to adhere the design positively to the screen. Use your hands to uniformly adhere the design positively to the screen. The oil will also render the unprinted portion of the paper translucent.
  • After establishing the positive, the screen will be exposed to a 40-watt LED light situated at a distance of 20 feet for 6 minutes. After exposing the panel for 6 minutes, you will wash it. It removes extra photo emulsion from the design. Examine the patterns for scums or oil that are obstructing them. Finally, you will be able to create a screen stencil. Then, using a cotton rug, wipe any extra water from the stencil.
  • Then, let the picture hardener dry. To avoid paint leaks, use tape at the corners of your screen stencil. You may now begin producing the mylar bags after pasting your screen stencil.

Finally, the silk screen imprinting on mylar bags is complete. Since you are taking a DIY approach, you will not be using professional equipment such as a conveyor dryer for the freezing process. Instead, you choose to dry your items in the sun or under sunlight. Although it takes time, printing in modest numbers at home becomes simple and cost-effective.
